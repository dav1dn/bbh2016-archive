what's a bay bit hack?

We're the first collegiate hackathon dedicated to blockchain and cryptocurrencies. Spend a weekend with us and join the ranks of a rapidly growing number of outstanding developers and entrepeneurs building on the future of the way we look at money. No experience with bitcoin or cryptocurrency technology is needed.

but wait! what's a bitcoin?

Bitcoin is a currency. It's a technology. It's a protocol of the future.

Bitcoin is a decentralized peer to peer online currency that can be sent and received by anyone in the world, relying on computers to control the transfer and creation of money without human intervention. This results in negligible transaction fees, no government corruption, and lightning-speed transfer of your digital property.

Who can attend Bay BitHack?

All students (undergraduate, graduate, or high school students over the age of 18) may attend Bay BitHack. We welcome students from all disciplines.

What should we bring?

Yourself, a student or government ID, your laptop, chargers, and anything else you need for 24 hours of development.

Do we need a team or an idea?

Since the world of cryptocurrencies is so new, we don't expect you to come with an idea or teammates. We'll have workshops and time to help you decide what you're going to work on.

How much does it cost?

About 3 satoshis. Just kidding, Bay BitHack is free and open to all students. We'll provide you with sustenance and a space to work for the weekend.

What kinds of things can I make?

All types of projects involving Bitcoin, cryptocurrencies, or the blockchain are welcome. Feel free to make the next big remittances solution, or a new exchange, a novel bitcoin wallet, or integrate the blockchain into the Internet of Things!

What's your code of conduct?

We abide by the Major League Hacking guidelines for conduct.

How can I contact you?

Please email us at info@baybithack.com if you have any questions or concerns. We'll get back to you as soon as we can!
